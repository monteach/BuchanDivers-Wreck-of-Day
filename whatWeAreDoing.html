What we are doing

6 May 2025 More research of WW2 U-boat logs has finally given us a name for the 'Knock Wreck' just off Fraserburgh. She is a Swedish vessel Svarton sunk by the U-58 in January 1940.
5 May 2025 After more research we now believe the wreck which we had identified as the Ethel Gwendoline is in fact the Parkhill. It is likely that the Ethel Gwendoline is in deep water about 6 miles east of Rattray Head. We have added the Parkhill to the website.
4 May 2025 Added the unknown trawler, which we discovered in 2023, to the website. We will need more work to confirm its identity but we believe she is the Caersin.
30 April 2025 After an almost two year break, we returned to exploration this year and visited a new mark just off Aberdeen near to the two HM Trawlers found in 2023. We believe this wreck to be the steam trawler Virgilia.
22 Jun 2023 Returned to the SS Bretagne again today, armed with a list of activities to determine if she is, in fact, the SS Malmland. It seems though, our original identification is correct! She is the Bretagne. The hunt for the Malmland continues.
26 Jun 2023 Dived a new mark today, off the mouth of the Ythan. Only in 40m, and very silty. We had believed this to be a WWI loss, an admiralty water tanker, but it turned out to be a reasonably well preserved steam trawler. We have work to do to identify it. A lovely dive though.
24 Jun 2023 Dived the wreck of the SS Bretagne today to attempt further identification. One huge boiler seen, but close to the bow and next to a massive boiler cradle, perhaps for another boiler - which would put our original identification into question. We will revisit for more investigation tomorrow.
31 May 2023 We couldn't dive our intended target today so selected to return to HMT Ormonde to try to get a more positive ID. The webpage will be updated with new photographs and dive information.
30 May 2023 We visited the site of the Ethel Gwendoline today. A more detailed sonar survey of the wreck had indicated a 2nd hull beside the first one. We were unable to find any trace of a 2nd hull, but we found information which means we need to go back to the drawing board on the identification of the wreck. She can't be the Ethel Gwendoline.
27 May 2023 A further work-up day, we visited the St Magnus. In lovely vis, we were able to get a good look at her and discovered a possible further clue to her demise. Look out for updates to her website entry.
26 May 2023 Our season started yesterday with a work up dive on the SS Wrangler to get back in practice. All went well and we spotted a very interesting mark, looking like a small puffer or steam fishing vessel which we returned to explore today. It turned out to be a perfectly shaped granite reef!
9 Aug 2022 Another super dive today in great conditions. We set out to dive the southerly mark of the "New Triangle" wrecks and found the HMT Robert Bowen, lost 9/2/1940 with all hands during the action which sank the HMT Fort Royal
8 Aug 2022 After a few days off due to boat problems we hit the seas again in perfect conditions. We dived the wreck known as the 33-Fathom to do more work to identify her. It is pretty certain from her cargo, size, general arrangement and cylinder diameters that she is the SS Dungonnell lost without trace on a voyage from Shields to Belfast in 1903.
26 Jul 2022 Updated the website with reports on the two new wrecks discovered this month. The SS Kathleen and the WW2 armed trawler, HMT Fort Royal.
22 Jul 2022 We dived the westerly mark of a group of three wrecks 8 miles off Balmedie. A great dive in super visibility. We found the remains of an armed trawler and are working on her identification. More soon.
21 Jul 2022 We returned to the unknown wreck referred to as the 33 Fathom to make a further attempt at identification. We did not find anything definitive on our dive but have a much more detailed understanding of her structure and cargo now, which will help another attempt to identify her.
19 Jul 2022 We have positively identified the wreck first dived on 14 July as the Crisabelle Stephen and have added this to the website. We also returned to the Hydrographic Office mark dived on 15th July and retrieved the ship's bell which identified her as the SS Kathleen, lost at sea with all crew in January 1914
15 Jul 2022 It's been an excruciatingly slow start to the season! A combination of boat problems, poor weather and other holidays meant that we started late. However, in the last couple of days we have dived two new marks off of the river Ythan, the 24 Fathom mark and a Hydrographic Office mark in 74m. Lovely wrecks, we are in the process of assessing all the information we collected on the dive in an attempt to name them. More soon.
2 Sept 2021 We have a confirmed find of the U 77. Very exciting! After weeks lost to diving through poor weather and now boat problems, we managed to get out on a mark and took video footage from the surface. This footage confirms the wreck of a submarine lying 100m below us. From research conducted by Kevin Heath and Michael Lowrey, this can only be the U 77 lost in 1916.
25 Jul 2021 The weather has not been kind to us in the last week. Great sea conditions, but fog and mist every day has made diving impossible. On the only diveable day, we attempted to dive the Knock Wreck to try to find more clues to her identity but strong spring tides made that impossible. However, today was perfect, glassy seas and sun! We dived the Taylor. A lovely small, but very intact steamer. We have new photos on the wreck page.
20 Jul 2021 Perfect conditions for the long trip south to dive "Hunks Big Wreck" which we have long suspected to be the Trevorian. A lovely dive, no absolute positive ID but number and design of boilers, plus size of wreck makes it highly likely that she is the Trevorian.
2 Jul 2021 An exciting trip to a new mark from the hydrographic survey. A "steamship" in 70m, rising 8m from seabed, 60m long. Very excited to see what it was. We arrived in perfect glassy seas to have a wonderful dive on a rock reef! Not another wreck to add to our list.
24 Jun 2021 A long trip south to dive the unidentified 33 Fathom for further exploration. Unfortunately our shot was off the wreck and we were unable to find it in the poor visibility in this area.
23 Jun 2021 A lovely dive on the Windward Ho I had forgotten just how intact she is. Her bows are intact and her hull has a good 3-4m height here.
18 Jun 2021 Dived the Sofie Bakke for the first time in many years. A grand old lady who hasn't changed much in the interval. We mainly dived around her massive diesel engines. Some new photos on the page.
17 Jun 2021 We returned to the St Fergus for further exploration. She is not broken in two as previously thought, however significant damage to bow area. Updates on the page.
5/6 June 2021 Bad weather and equipment failures over last couple of weeks kept us from visiting the St Fergus again, however we managed to get in on the SS Simonburn and the lovely SS Muriel. Super dives, but the season has had a typically weather interrupted start!
17/18 May 2021 Dived SS Cairnmona and HMS Flotta in fantastic visibility. Pages have been updated with new pictures by Naomi Watson.
22 April 2021 Diving has restarted for this year with a work-up dive on the SS Cairnmona. We dived in the area of the bridge and found a lovely telegraph, the helm and lots of port-holes.
11 April 2021 Sounded a new mark in 103m north of Fraserburgh. We think this will be a very special wreck and are working up to dive it and confirm its identity later in the season.
11 Sept 2020 We explored a new wreck in 75m, 5 miles east of Rattray Head, we believe her to be the St Fergus but we will need to revisit her for further confirmation.
